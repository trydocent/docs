Docent is non-custodial. The goal is to make funding and balances easier to use without turning money movement into a black box.

## What this means in practice

You can deposit USDC on Base, Arbitrum, and Hyperliquid, then use Docent to trade supported venues from the same app.

Docent shows a simpler balance view, but your funds still live on the chain or venue where they are being used.

## Deposit where it is easiest for you

Docent supports deposits on:

- Base
- Arbitrum
- Hyperliquid

That gives you more than one path to fund your account before you trade.

## Trade on the venue you choose

Docent does not replace the trading venue. It helps you access the venue through a simpler app experience.

- When you trade perpetuals or spot, funds used for those trades live on `Hyperliquid`
- When you trade prediction markets, funds used for those trades live on `Polymarket`

If you deposit directly to Hyperliquid, use the `HyperCore` chain.

## See one balance without losing track

Docent shows a single balance in the app even when funds are spread across supported chains and trading venues.

That makes the product easier to use. It does not change where funds are actually held at a given moment.

## Wallet infrastructure

Docent uses `Privy` for wallet infrastructure and `OneBalance` for chain abstraction and routing.

`Privy` powers the wallet layer and account experience. `OneBalance` helps Docent present one balance and move funds across supported chains and venues with less manual setup.

## Keep control of your wallet

You can export your wallet key from Docent. Keep it private. Never share it with anyone.

<Warning>
  Docent will never ask you to send your private key to support or to another user.
</Warning>
